    5. Reservoirs: understand your flood risk

    Your selected location: 8, Sydenham Way, Dorchester, Dorset DT1 1DN

    This information tells you the flood risk of the land around a building, not the building itself.

    Flooding from reservoirs is unlikely in this area.

    What a reservoir is:

    A reservoir is a large natural or artificial lake that is designed to collect and store water.

    They are usually formed by building a dam across a river. If one of these dams fail then water could escape from the reservoir. This would result in land or properties being flooded.

    How we check an area's risk

    We use predicted scenarios to understand the risk of flooding from reservoirs.

    Flooding from reservoirs is extremely unlikely. We have an excellent reservoir safety record in the UK.

    An area is considered at risk if people's lives could be threatened in the event of a dam or reservoir failure. There has been no loss of life due to a dam failing in the UK since 1925.

    What to do

    • Make sure you can get emergency alerts from the government

    • look at a reservoir flood map to see where the water may go
    • it often happens in the places you don't expect, like on the side of a hill

    You should check your flood risk regularly because the information may change.
