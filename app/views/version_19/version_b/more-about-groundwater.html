    Why groundwater flooding is a problem

    Groundwater flooding is a problem because:

    • water will come up through the ground or the floor
    • It usually happens days, weeks or months after lots of rain and can last a long time
    • it often happens in the places you don't expect, like on the side of a hill
    How we assess an area's flood risk

    It is difficult to predict groundwater flooding. We use flood alert data to check the risk of flooding from groundwater.

    Groundwater flood alert areas are set up where there is good monitoring data along with historic flood data.

    Alert areas can cover large parts of the community - not every property within the area may be at risk of flooding.

    Some properties outside of flood alert areas may also be at risk of groundwater flooding.

    What you can do next

    You cannot completely protect your property from flooding, but there's a lot you can do to reduce:

    • the amount of water that gets in
    • the damage from flood water
    • the cost and time of cleaning up after a flood

    You can:

    You should check your flood risk regularly because the information may change.

    If you need more details on groundwater flood risk

    Lead local flood authorities are responsible for managing flood risk from groundwater. They may hold more detailed information.
