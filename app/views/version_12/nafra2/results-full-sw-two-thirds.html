What the flood risk ratings mean

'High' means more than 3.3% chance of a flood each year (more than 1 in 30 chance).

'Medium' means between 1% and 3.3% chance of a flood each year (between 1 in 100 and 1 in 30 chance).

'Low' means between 0.1% and 1% chance of a flood each year (between 1 in 1000 and 1 in 100 chance).

'Very low' means less than 0.1% chance of a flood each year (less than 1 in 1000 chance).

What you can do

An area can still be at risk of flooding even if it has not flooded in the past. It's a good idea to check your flood risk regularly because the information may change.

You can also contact your local council for advice on how to manage surface water flood risk.
