Nafra2 service iteration Version 11.0

Third iteration of service with Nafra2 data Tested

An A/B test primarily to see if removing stats on the summary page impacts users' understanding of the service.

V10 research findings

  • Navigation is fine, as are Rivers/Surface water maps
  • Users related to highlighted chance language more than statistics. They need more context (relative risk) to understand statistics
  • Users are somewhat confident in climate change data
  • Users expect context on 'see more details' page
  • Depth: users thought depth pages explained extent
  • Depth: 3 pages were overwhelming, the comparison page was the easiest to understand
  • Depth: users not sure if it was still related to the address originally searched for
  • Depth: 2 high risk ratings on gauge confused some users
V11.0 changes
  • Removed statistics on summary on one version to see if it reduces cognitive load
  • These stats have been shifted to the 'more details' page where there is a user expectation for more context of what the risk rating means
  • Changed link text length on summary as the length seemed to be impacting readability/visibility
  • Removed chance labels that aren't related to the address on more details as the range has been provided on the summary
  • Depth is now one page, which shows how deep any flood water could be, combining the aspects that worked well in the 3 step version; bolding the border of the most likely rating and having a comparison of the now and future with flood risk ratings only.
  • Depth now has more structure as a page, with what address the depths related to, a summary of the gauge, the gauge itself, what the impact of different depths are and what you can do about them - with a link to further guidance.
