What the flood risk ratings mean

'High' means more than 3.3% chance of a flood each year (or more than 50% chance of a flood over the next 20 years)

'Medium' means between 1% and 3.3% chance of a flood each year (or between 20% and 50% chance of a flood over the next 20 years)

'Low' means between 0.1% and 1% chance of a flood each year (or between 2% and 20% chance of a flood over the next 20 years)

'Very low' means less than 0.1% chance of a flood each year (or less than 2% chance of a flood over the next 20 years)

How climate change is affecting flood risk

Climate change is increasing the chances of flooding. Climate models project that we will see:

  • rising sea levels
  • warmer and wetter winters
  • more frequent and intense weather events

What you can do

It's a good idea to check your flood risk regularly because the information may change.

You can also:

  • check how deep any flood water could be
  • sign up to receive flood warnings by phone, text or email
  • read about how to prepare yourself and your property
