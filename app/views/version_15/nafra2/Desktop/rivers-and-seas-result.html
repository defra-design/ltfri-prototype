Why we cannot tell you the flood risk of a specific property
Our flood risk data does not include every feature like kerbs, or fences and walls around a building. That means we can only tell you the flood risk of the land around a building, not the building itself.

What makes rivers and sea flooding more likely

Low-lying areas that are close to rivers or the sea are more likely to flood when water levels rise.

This information takes into account any flood defences.

Why flood defences cannot completely prevent flooding

Flood defences can help reduce the chance of flooding but cannot completely prevent it because:

  • they can fail
  • water could spill over the top if it is deep enough

How likely a river or sea flood is

Climate change is increasing the chances of flooding. Climate models project that we will see:

  • warmer and wetter winters
  • more frequent and intense weather events

What the flood risk ratings mean

'High' means more than 3.3% chance of a flood each year

'Medium' means between 1% and 3.3% chance of a flood each year

'Low' means between 0.1% and 1% chance of a flood each year

'Very low' means less than 0.1% chance of a flood each year

How deep any flood water could be

Even a small amount of water can cause significant damage so it's a good idea to understand how deep any flood water could be.

You cannot completely protect your property from flooding, but there's a lot you can do to reduce:

  • the amount of flood water that gets in
  • the damage from flood water
  • the cost and time of cleaning up after a flood
