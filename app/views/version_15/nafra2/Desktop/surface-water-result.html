Why we cannot tell you the flood risk of a specific property
Our flood risk data does not include every feature like kerbs, or fences and walls around a building. That means we can only tell you the flood risk of the land around a building, not the building itself.

What surface water is

Surface water flooding is sometimes known as flash flooding. It happens when rainwater cannot drain away through normal drainage systems.

Why surface water is a problem

Surface water flooding is a problem because:

  • it often happens in places you don't expect, like up hills
  • it's hard to predict, as heavy rain sometimes only affects a small part of an area
  • where there's lots of tarmac and concrete the water has nowhere to go

How likely a surface water flood is

Climate change is increasing the chances of flooding. Climate models project that we will see:

  • warmer and wetter winters
  • more frequent and intense weather events

What the flood risk ratings mean

High

More than 3.3% chance of a flood each year

Medium

Between 1% and 3.3% chance of a flood each year

Low

Between 0.1% and 1% chance of a flood each year

Very low

Less than 0.1% chance of a flood each year

You cannot completely protect your property from flooding, but there's a lot you can do to reduce:

  • the amount of flood water that gets in
  • the damage from flood water
  • the cost and time of cleaning up after a flood
