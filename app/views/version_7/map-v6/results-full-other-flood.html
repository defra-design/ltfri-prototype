More details about other flood risks

GroundwaterFlooding is possible when groundwater levels are high

What groundwater is
Groundwater is the water that is usually held in rocks and soil underground. 

Groundwater flooding happens when this water rises and flows above the surface.
How we check an area's risk
We use flood alert data to check the risk of flooding from groundwater..

What you can do
Use the 'check for flooding' service to check current groundwater levels.

ReservoirsFlooding from reservoirs is extremely unlikely

What is a reservoir
Reservoirs are large natural or artificial lakes which collect and store water. 

They're built with safety measures in place to prevent overflow and flooding.
How we check an area's risk
We use predicted scenarios to understand the risk of flooding from reservoirs.

An area is considered to be at risk when people's lives could be in danger if a dam or reservoir breaks.
What you can do
View the map of the risk of reservoir flooding to see information on where any flood water might spread to:
  • when river levels are normal
  • when there is also flooding from nearby rivers
