Managing flood risk

Our climate is changing and one of the risks is an increase in flooding. Extreme weather events could happen more often.

You can still be at risk of flooding even if your area has not flooded in the past.

Check flood warnings

  • View live flood warnings
  • sign up for flood warnings

Plan ahead

Plan how you'll respond to a flood by making a:

  • personal flood plan
  • community or group flood plan
  • business flood plan

Protect your property

Take action to ensure your property has the best available protection against flooding:

  • Get advice from The National Flood Forum about how to protect your property and how much this will cost
  • Find flood protection products and services at Blue Pages
  • Find out about local schemes and strategies to manage flood risk in your area
  • Use nature-based solutions to reduce flooding in your area

Get insurance

Make sure you have insurance that covers you for flood risk.

You can:

  • get insurance advice from the National Flood Forum
  • find lower-cost home insurance at Flood Re if you're in a flood-risk area
  • find a broker at The British Insurance Broker's Association that specialises in properties that are difficult to insure
  • request a flooding history report to find out if a property is in an area which has been flooded in the past
  • request an insurance related report for evidence of your flood risk for insurance purposes
  • complete a flood risk report to show how the work you have done on your property has reduced your flood risk
