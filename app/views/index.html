V14.0 May to June 2024
V14.0 changes

Summary

  • Changed heading from 'Yearly chance of flooding with climate change' to 'Yearly chance of flooding by the 2050s'.
  • Also added two new dropdowns 'What we mean by the 2050s' in the Surface water and the Rivers and sea sections. The information inside them is different to reflect the different time periods

Surface water: understand your flood risk

  • Removed the link 'increasing the chances of flooding' under the climate change information
  • Also reduced the content in 'What the risk ratings mean' dropdown

Surface water: possible flood depths

  • Added in graphs to cover each depth level of 20, 30 and 60cm across present day and the 2050s timelines
  • Included an individual risk rating of Very low, Low, Medium or High on the present day and 2050s graphs
  • Created two new versions A tabs and B stacked layout
V13.0 March 2024 to May 2024
V13.0 changes
  • Summary: changed 'by 2050s' to 'with climate change', potentially removing the need to explain why the 2050s have been chosen on the summary page. Also made information and flood defences more prominent.
  • More details: created one line of flood risk now and in the future with the wording 'increasing to' to imply gradual change over time.
  • More details: added context about climate change and guidance page link.
  • More details: testing a new way of presenting absolute risk as 1 in X is misinterpreted. Using a 20 year period and rounded up percentages to show what these initial percentages (1%/3.3%) mean over 20 years (20%/50%)
  • Depth: stripped back to give the gist of the information. Not working quite how it's modelled, if a flood were to happen then these are the likely depths and how they're changing by 2050s. Using 'Present day' and '2050s' as we've already spoken about yearly chance on the 'more details' page and it is implied.
V12.0 February 2024 to March 2024
V12.0 changes
  • Summary page: reduced width of cards to keep them more contained, added more visual hierarchy, added a line to clarify what the highest risk is to both highlight which risk to focus on and imply there are more (for some users who don't scroll all the way down).
  • Using 'Yearly' language after feedback from UCD community.
  • Redesign of 'more details' page to use a more standard design to differentiate it from the summary page where the card design makes sense in breaking up similar flood risks.
  • 'More details' page now has better structure and readibility. Combined the flood risks in one line, which means that the drop down 'What the flood risk ratings mean' is now in the right section as it pertains to both ratings.
  • Depth is now part of 'what you can do' as it is linked to guidance about how you can protect your home once you understand how deep flooding might be.
V11.0 January 2024 to February 2024
V11.0 changes
  • Removed statistics on summary on one version to see if it reduces cognitive load
  • These stats have been shifted to the 'more details' page where there is a user expectation for more context of what the risk rating means
  • Changed link text length on summary as the length seemed to be impacting readability/visibility
  • Removed chance labels that aren't related to the address on more details as the range has been provided on the summary
  • Depth is now one page, which shows how deep any flood water could be, combining the aspects that worked well in the 3 step version; bolding the border of the most likely rating and having a comparison of the now and future with flood risk ratings only.
  • Depth now has more structure as a page, with what address the depths related to, a summary of the gauge, the gauge itself, what the impact of different depths are and what you can do about them - with a link to further guidance.
