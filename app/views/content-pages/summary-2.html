Chance of flooding
The chance of flooding in a high risk area is more than 1 in 30 in any one year.

What makes an area more likely to flood from surface water
Surface water flooding is sometimes known as flash flooding. It happens when rainwater cannot drain away through normal drainage systems.

It can:
  • be difficult to predict as it depends on how much it rains and where
  • happen up hills and away from rivers
  • be worse in areas which have harder surfaces, like concrete
Potential depth of water
It is difficult to predict the exact depth of any flood water.

In a 'high risk' flood, computer models show the water would probably be shallower than 30cm. In some places it could be deeper.
Managing your flood risk
An area can still be at risk of flooding even if it has not flooded in the past.

There are things everyone can do to manage their risk. You should:
  • find out what you can do to protect you and your property
  • contact your local council for advice on managing surface water flood risk
You can also view a map of the risk of surface water flooding which shows an estimation of:
  • where any flood water might spread to
  • how deep any flood water could be
What this information covers
This information tells you the highest chance of flooding on the land around a property.

It is not specific to a property.

What you can use this information for
The information is an estimate based on the best data we have available. It is suitable for identifying:
  • which parts of towns or streets are at risk, or have the most risk
  • the approximate extent and depth of flooding
It's likely to be reliable for a local area but not for identifying individual properties at risk.
