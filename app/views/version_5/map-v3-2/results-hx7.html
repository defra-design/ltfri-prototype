Surface waterHigh risk of flooding

  • View a map of surface water flooding
How likely a flood is
Each year there is more than a 1 in 30 chance of flooding in a high risk area.

What makes an area more likely to flood from surface water

Surface water flooding is sometimes known as flash flooding. It happens when rainwater cannot drain away through normal drainage systems.

It can:

  • be difficult to predict as it depends on how much it rains and where
  • happen up hills and away from rivers
  • be worse in areas which have harder surfaces, like concrete
How deep a flood could be
It is difficult to predict the exact depth of any flood water.

Flood models show the water would probably be shallower than 30cm. Deeper flooding cannot be ruled out in extreme weather.

How likely a flood is
Each year there is between a 1 in 30 and 1 in 100 chance of flooding in a medium risk area.

What makes an area more likely to flood from rivers and the sea
Low-lying areas that are close to rivers or the sea are more likely to flood when water levels rise.
How deep a flood could be
At the moment we do not have information about the predicted depth of flood water from rivers and the sea.
Manage your flood risk
You should:
  • sign up to receive flood warnings by phone, text or email
  • find out what you can do to protect your property
  • check your long term flood risk every 12 months because the information may change

View the map to see an estimation of where any flood water might spread to.

What this information covers
This information tells you the highest chance of flooding on the land around a property. It is not specific to a property.

The information takes into account any flood defences. They can help reduce the chance of flooding but cannot completely prevent it because:
  • they can fail
  • water could spill over the top if it is deep enough
What you can use this information for
The information is an estimate based on the best data we have available, which is a combination of:
  • predicted river flows, rainfall and coastal levels
  • information about the physical features of an area, including hills, valleys, and rivers
This information is suitable for identifying:
  • which parts of countries or counties are at risk, or have the most risk
  • areas likely to flood first, deepest or most frequently

It's very unlikely to be reliable for a local area. It is extremely unlikely to be reliable for identifying individual properties at risk.

GroundwaterFlooding is possible when groundwater levels are high

Use the 'check for flooding' service to check current groundwater levels.

What groundwater is and how we check an area's risk
Groundwater is the water that is usually held in rocks and soil underground. Groundwater flooding happens when this water rises and flows above the surface.

We use flood alert data to check the risk of flooding from groundwater.

What a reservoir is and how we check an area's risk

Reservoirs are large natural or artificial lakes which collect and store water. They're built with safety measures in place to prevent overflow and flooding.

We use predicted scenarios to understand the risk of flooding from reservoirs.

Flooding from reservoirs is extremely unlikely. An area is considered at risk if peoples' lives could be threatened in the event of a dam or reservoir failure.
