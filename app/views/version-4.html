Research insights from maps discoveryFebruary to April 2023

Users cannot easily find the key on the map and language on the key can be confusing​

The title of the map is difficult to identify and does not clearly communicate the map information in a way that is accessible to all users​

Users are not able to easily switch between different views of the map – current drop down does not support clarity​

The colours on the map can be confusing for some users, particularly in the following areas:​

  • Distinguishing between flooding and other water​
  • Differentiating the different shades of blue​
  • Thinking about other factors like roads vs flood colours​

Citizens do not tend to engage with flooding and knowledge can be limited, therefore there is a job to do to support users in accessing flood services – particularly those who may be living in flood risk areas without knowing​

Other research findings

  • Users need context to be able to understand what the information means in the real world​​
  • Language can limit engagement ​
  • Citizens can be overwhelmed with the volume of information shown on the service

V4.0 research insights summary
  • Changing the map interaction has not changed understanding (expectedly), and issues with understanding continue to present a problem for what users take from the service
  • Whilst using the map, users were able to navigate between layers in both the full and partial screen versions​
  • Radio buttons are optimal for user interaction ​
  • However, the radio buttons show the different options visibly highlighting everything available to users which may be overwhelming for some users​
  • Users switched between the layers to gain understanding​
  • A combined view of flood extent (high/medium/low) could provide context​
  • Base map features unclear to users​
  • Full screen works better – move forward with this prototype​
